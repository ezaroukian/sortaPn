Instructions 1/3

In this HIT, you will be interpreting hedges, which are words like "sorta", "kinda", "like", and "-ish":

  • He sorta did a cartwheel.
  • I kinda forgot.
  • She read like twenty articles.
  • It was blue-ish.
Speakers can use a hedge to indicate a mismatch between what they are saying and what they actually mean.

A hedge can indicate a mismatch in various parts of a sentence, as will be described to you through examples on the next page.
